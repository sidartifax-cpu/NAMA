Our Story

A Century of Black
Music in Harlem

NAMA is not just a venue — it is a living piece of American music history. For over 120 years, the New Amsterdam Musical Association has been a cornerstone of Black culture in Harlem, New York City.

1904

NAMA Founded in Harlem

The New Amsterdam Musical Association is established — the first African-American music union, welcoming Black musicians excluded from Local 310.

1920s

Harlem Renaissance Home

NAMA becomes a social and musical hub during the Harlem Renaissance. Legendary members including James Reese Europe pass through its doors.

1990s

Community Revival & Programs

NAMA relaunches community programming — open jams, educational events, and the Blues & Poetry series that carry its legacy into the modern era.

2022

Centennial Celebration & Historic Landmark

NAMA marks 100 years at the same Harlem address. The brownstone is celebrated as a Historic Landmark and renovation efforts begin.

Today

Still Swinging in Harlem

Weekly events, community programs, and an active renovations campaign — NAMA continues its mission for generations of artists to come.

New Amsterdam Musical Association

Founded in 1904, NAMA is the oldest African-American musical organization in the United States. Headquartered in the heart of Harlem for over 120 years, we are dedicated to the promotion and preservation of Black musical heritage.
